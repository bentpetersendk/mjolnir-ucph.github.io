Research computing for the Globe Institute

Mjolnir is an HPC cluster funded by a consortium of principal investigators affiliated with the Globe Institute at the University of Copenhagen. The cluster is housed at UCPH and integrated into its IT infrastructure.

Access is restricted to UCPH employees and external collaborators associated with the consortium.

Consortium-based access

Mjolnir is organized around participating principal investigators and the research groups connected to them.

What Mjolnir provides

Mjolnir gives researchers access to shared compute capacity, scientific software, and support channels for analyses that outgrow local machines.

High-performance computing

Compute resources for large research workloads and powerful data analysis.

Scientific software

More than 1,400 installed software packages and scientific tools are available to users.

UCPH integration

The cluster is housed at UCPH and integrated into University IT infrastructure.

Research group access

Access follows consortium affiliation and principal investigator responsibility.

Software requests

Users can request tools and databases that are not yet available on Mjolnir.

Support and documentation

Support ticketing and knowledgebase resources help users get started and resolve issues.

Built for big research questions

The name Mjolnir refers to Thor's hammer, chosen here as a symbol for processing vast amounts of data and tackling research analyses at scale.
